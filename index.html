Your game title

  • High Concept: Avoid the dodgeballs for as long as you can. Your character follows the mouse.
  • Player Experience Goals: A slowly incrementing difficulty that changes seemlessly from easy to incredibly hard, and gets harder and harder the longer the player can last.
  • Genre: Arcade/Survival
  • Game play: The player can dodge the balls by moving the mouse. Sticking to a corner could be a solid tactic, but moving around allows more freedom of maneuverability.
  • Prototype goal: Mouse aversion.
  • Features: While some games typically make difficulty harder by levels, waves, etc., this game is made difficult constantly over time, rather than by an increment every few minutes.
  • Setting/Premise: You're playing as Ed, he's in gym class and is trying to avoid being knocked out in an increasingly difficult dodgeball game.
  • Story: It's an arcade game; there is very little story besides the above.
  • Target Audience: Casuals.
  • Play time: Anywhere between a few seconds to a few minutes depending on how skilled the player is.
  • Strategies: Hugging corners for more experienced players who figure out how to last a decent amount of time by not moving, and weaving in an out of the predicted patterns of the dodgeballs for others.
  • Prototype Assets: I used the template model for the movement of Ed.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Robotron
